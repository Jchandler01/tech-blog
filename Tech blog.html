Humans Need Not Apply

Jack Chandler

The video "Humans need not apply" talks about how humans in the near future will loose their jobs due to the rapid increase of new technology. Dozens of jobs, including transportation, constrution labor, and even medical jobs, will inevitably be taken over by robots. Even the jobs that involve creativity and human emotion will be taken over, because robots are constantly growing and learning. Many people believe that this technology is very far away, however this technology actually exists now. While jobs won't be completely taken over right now it will eventully happen.

This is a huge problem that the human race is not prepared for. The people in power need to face the inevitable truth that robots will be leaving millions of people unemployed and we need to come up with a solution fast.

Interview

By: Jack Chandler

I interviewed my Mother on her opinons on technology including what it was like when she was my age and how she feels technology is changing

Me: How old were you when you got your first computer?

Janet: 36

Me: What was your favorite piece of technology when you were growing up?

Janet: TV

Me: How has technology changed over your lifetime?

Janet: It has changed a lot! Biggest change is mobile phones. Quick access to information, emails in the palm of your hand. When I was growing up I would write a letter to a friend that was far away and wait a week or so for a reply. Now I can text and geta reply in a instant.

Me: Have the changes you've seen in technology over your lifetime mostly helped or society?

Janet: Both! It is definetly really good to stay in contact with family and friends. Also really conveniant to have quick access to information. But it has been bad because now we are all of obsessed with our phones. Maybe even addicted to them.

Me: What is your greatest fear for technology in the future?

Janet: That technology will help solve some of the worlds problems such as climate change.

Me: What advice would you like to share with the younger generation about technology?

Janet: Connecting to yourself and to other peolpe is more important then connecting to technoolgy.

Me: Do you think technology will continue to change?

Janet: Yes! I can't imagine what technology is going to look like when you are my age. It's a little scary!
